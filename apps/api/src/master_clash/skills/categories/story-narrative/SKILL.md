# Story & Narrative Video Production

## When to Use
- Brand storytelling
- Short films
- Documentary style content
- Emotional marketing
- Origin stories

## Narrative Structures

### Three-Act Structure
1. **Setup (25%)** - Establish world, characters, stakes
2. **Confrontation (50%)** - Conflict, obstacles, development
3. **Resolution (25%)** - Climax and emotional payoff

### Hero's Journey (Simplified)
1. Ordinary world
2. Call to adventure
3. Challenges and allies
4. The ordeal
5. Transformation
6. Return changed

### Brand Story Framework
1. **Once upon a time...** - The founder/origin
2. **Every day...** - The status quo problem
3. **But one day...** - The insight/breakthrough
4. **Because of that...** - The solution created
5. **Until finally...** - The impact achieved

## Visual Storytelling

### Shot Progression
- **Wide → Medium → Close** for intimacy building
- **Close → Wide** for revelation/context
- **Match cuts** for thematic connections
- **Montage** for time passage

### Emotional Beats
| Emotion | Visual Approach |
|---------|-----------------|
| Wonder | Wide shots, low angle, golden light |
| Tension | Tight framing, shadows, quick cuts |
| Joy | Bright colors, movement, faces |
| Nostalgia | Warm tones, soft focus, slow motion |
| Triumph | Dynamic angles, rising movement |

### Prompt Templates

**Establishing Mood:**
```
Cinematic [location] scene, [time of day] lighting,
[emotion] atmosphere, film photography style,
anamorphic lens flare, [color palette] color grade
```

**Character Moment:**
```
[Character description] in [emotional state],
portrait photography, natural lighting,
shallow depth of field, storytelling composition,
authentic moment captured, cinematic still
```

## Audio Considerations
- Music arc should match story arc
- Sound design for immersion
- Strategic use of silence
- Voice-over pacing with visuals
